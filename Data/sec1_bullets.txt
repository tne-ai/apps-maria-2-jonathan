To analyze the Gross Regional Product (GRP) of San Diego County between 2019 and 2023, we will examine the data provided in the CSV files. The analysis will focus on the total GRP, per capita GRP, and the performance of various industries over the specified period.

### Total GRP Analysis:
1. **2019**: The total GRP for San Diego County was approximately $244.28 billion.
2. **2020**: The GRP slightly increased to approximately $244.82 billion, despite the economic challenges posed by the COVID-19 pandemic.
3. **2021**: The GRP saw a significant increase to approximately $268.87 billion, indicating a strong economic recovery.
4. **2022**: The GRP continued to grow, reaching approximately $296.68 billion.
5. **2023**: The GRP further increased to approximately $308.71 billion.

### Per Capita GRP Analysis:
1. **2019**: The per capita GRP was approximately $73,347.
2. **2020**: The per capita GRP increased to approximately $74,278.
3. **2021**: The per capita GRP rose significantly to approximately $82,100.
4. **2022**: The per capita GRP further increased to approximately $90,557.
5. **2023**: The per capita GRP reached approximately $94,916.

### Industry Performance:
- **Accommodation and Food Services**: This industry experienced fluctuations, with a decline in 2020 due to the pandemic, followed by a recovery in subsequent years.
- **Administrative and Support and Waste Management and Remediation Services**: This sector showed consistent growth over the years.
- **Agriculture, Forestry, Fishing and Hunting**: This industry had a steady increase in GRP contribution.
- **Arts, Entertainment, and Recreation**: This sector was heavily impacted in 2020 but showed recovery in the following years.
- **Construction**: The construction industry demonstrated steady growth throughout the period.
- **Finance and Insurance**: This sector showed significant growth, especially in 2020 and 2021.
- **Government**: The government sector consistently contributed the largest share to the GRP.
- **Health Care and Social Assistance**: This industry showed steady growth, reflecting its essential role during the pandemic.
- **Information**: The information sector experienced growth, particularly in 2022 and 2023.
- **Manufacturing**: Manufacturing remained a strong contributor to the GRP, with consistent growth.
- **Professional, Scientific, and Technical Services**: This sector showed robust growth, particularly in 2022 and 2023.
- **Real Estate and Rental and Leasing**: This industry showed steady growth over the years.
- **Retail Trade**: Retail trade experienced growth, with a notable increase in 2023.
- **Transportation and Warehousing**: This sector showed growth, particularly in 2022 and 2023.
- **Utilities**: The utilities sector showed moderate growth over the period.
- **Wholesale Trade**: This industry demonstrated consistent growth throughout the years.

### Conclusion:
San Diego County's GRP showed a strong upward trend from 2019 to 2023, with significant growth in per capita GRP. The economic recovery post-2020 was robust, with several industries, particularly finance, manufacturing, and professional services, contributing significantly to the growth. The data indicates a resilient and diverse economy capable of overcoming challenges and sustaining growth.
