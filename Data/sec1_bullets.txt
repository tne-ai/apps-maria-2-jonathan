The Gross Regional Product (GRP) analysis for San Diego County from 2019 to 2023 is as follows:

1. **2019**:
   - Total GRP: $244.28 billion
   - Population: 3,330,458
   - GRP per Capita: $73,346.92

2. **2020**:
   - Total GRP: $244.82 billion
   - Population: 3,296,045
   - GRP per Capita: $74,277.60
   - Analysis: Despite the challenges posed by the COVID-19 pandemic, San Diego County's GRP saw a slight increase from 2019. The GRP per capita also increased, indicating a resilience in economic output relative to the population.

3. **2021**:
   - Total GRP: $268.87 billion
   - Population: 3,274,954
   - GRP per Capita: $82,099.95
   - Analysis: There was a significant increase in GRP, reflecting economic recovery and growth post-pandemic. The GRP per capita also saw a substantial rise, suggesting improved economic productivity and output per person.

4. **2022**:
   - Total GRP: $296.68 billion
   - Population: 3,276,208
   - GRP per Capita: $90,557.10
   - Analysis: The GRP continued to grow robustly, with a notable increase in both total GRP and GRP per capita. This indicates strong economic performance and an increase in the economic value generated per resident.

5. **2023**:
   - Total GRP: $308.71 billion
   - Population: 3,252,468
   - GRP per Capita: $94,915.87
   - Analysis: The GRP reached its highest level in this period, with a continued increase in GRP per capita. This suggests sustained economic growth and efficiency in San Diego County's economy.

**Overall Analysis**:
- Over the five-year period, San Diego County's GRP increased from $244.28 billion in 2019 to $308.71 billion in 2023, marking a growth of approximately 26.4%.
- The GRP per capita also rose significantly from $73,346.92 in 2019 to $94,915.87 in 2023, indicating enhanced economic productivity and output per individual.
- The data reflects a resilient and growing economy in San Diego County, with consistent increases in both total GRP and GRP per capita, despite the challenges posed by the pandemic in 2020.
