The Gross Regional Product (GRP) of San Diego County from 2019 to 2023 shows a consistent upward trend, indicating economic growth over this period. Here's a detailed analysis:

1. **Overall GRP Growth**:
   - In 2019, the GRP was approximately $244.28 billion.
   - By 2023, the GRP increased to approximately $308.71 billion.
   - This represents an increase of about $64.43 billion over the five-year period.

2. **Annual GRP Growth**:
   - From 2019 to 2020, the GRP increased slightly from $244.28 billion to $244.82 billion, showing resilience despite the global economic challenges posed by the COVID-19 pandemic.
   - From 2020 to 2021, the GRP saw a significant increase to $268.87 billion, indicating a strong recovery.
   - The growth continued in 2022, with the GRP reaching $296.68 billion.
   - In 2023, the GRP further increased to $308.71 billion.

3. **Per Capita GRP**:
   - The per capita GRP also showed growth, reflecting not just an increase in total economic output but also an improvement in economic productivity per person.
   - In 2019, the per capita GRP was approximately $73,347.
   - By 2023, it had increased to approximately $94,916.
   - This indicates a per capita increase of about $21,569 over the five years.

4. **Comparison with State and National Levels**:
   - San Diego County's per capita GRP in 2023 ($94,916) was higher than both the state of California ($93,800) and the national average ($77,366), highlighting the county's strong economic performance relative to broader benchmarks.

5. **Sector Contributions**:
   - Key sectors contributing to the GRP include Government, Professional, Scientific, and Technical Services, and Manufacturing, which consistently showed strong performance throughout the years.
   - The Government sector, in particular, was the largest contributor, with its GRP increasing from $45.19 billion in 2019 to $52.92 billion in 2023.

Overall, San Diego County's economy demonstrated robust growth from 2019 to 2023, with significant contributions from key sectors and a strong per capita performance compared to state and national levels.
