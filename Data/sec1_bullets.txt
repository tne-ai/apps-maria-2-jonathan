The Gross Regional Product (GRP) of San Diego County from 2019 to 2023 shows a consistent upward trend, indicating economic growth over the period. Here's a detailed analysis:

1. **Overall GRP Growth**:
   - In 2019, the GRP was approximately $244.28 billion.
   - By 2023, the GRP had increased to approximately $308.71 billion.
   - This represents an increase of about $64.43 billion over the five-year period.

2. **Annual GRP Figures**:
   - 2019: $244.28 billion
   - 2020: $244.82 billion
   - 2021: $268.87 billion
   - 2022: $296.68 billion
   - 2023: $308.71 billion

3. **Yearly Growth Rates**:
   - From 2019 to 2020, the GRP increased by approximately 0.22%.
   - From 2020 to 2021, the GRP grew by approximately 9.82%.
   - From 2021 to 2022, the GRP increased by approximately 10.34%.
   - From 2022 to 2023, the GRP grew by approximately 4.05%.

4. **Per Capita GRP**:
   - The per capita GRP also showed an upward trend, indicating that the economic growth was not solely due to population growth.
   - 2019: $73,346.92
   - 2020: $74,277.60
   - 2021: $82,099.95
   - 2022: $90,557.10
   - 2023: $94,915.87

5. **Key Observations**:
   - The most significant growth in GRP occurred between 2020 and 2022, with double-digit growth rates, likely reflecting recovery and expansion following the economic impacts of the COVID-19 pandemic.
   - The per capita GRP growth suggests improvements in productivity and economic output per individual in the region.

Overall, San Diego County experienced robust economic growth from 2019 to 2023, with both total GRP and per capita GRP increasing significantly. This growth reflects a strong regional economy with potential contributions from various sectors, including technology, tourism, and defense, which are prominent in the area.
