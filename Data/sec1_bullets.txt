**Gross Regional Product (GRP) Analysis of San Diego County (2019-2023)**

**Introduction:**
The Gross Regional Product (GRP) is a vital economic indicator that measures the economic performance of a region. This report provides an analysis of the GRP for San Diego County from 2019 to 2023, highlighting key trends and sectoral contributions.

**Overall GRP Growth:**
San Diego County's GRP has shown a consistent upward trend over the five-year period from 2019 to 2023. The total GRP increased from approximately $244.28 billion in 2019 to $308.71 billion in 2023. This represents a compound annual growth rate (CAGR) of approximately 6.0%.

**Yearly GRP Figures:**
- **2019:** $244.28 billion
- **2020:** $244.82 billion
- **2021:** $268.87 billion
- **2022:** $296.68 billion
- **2023:** $308.71 billion

**Sectoral Contributions:**
1. **Government:** The government sector consistently contributed the largest share to the GRP, with its contribution increasing from $45.19 billion in 2019 to $52.92 billion in 2023. This sector's growth reflects its stability and essential role in the regional economy.

2. **Manufacturing:** Manufacturing was a significant contributor, with its GRP rising from $28.26 billion in 2019 to $31.67 billion in 2023. This sector's growth underscores its importance in driving economic activity and employment in the region.

3. **Professional, Scientific, and Technical Services:** This sector experienced substantial growth, with its contribution increasing from $28.13 billion in 2019 to $37.04 billion in 2023. The sector's expansion highlights the region's strength in innovation and technology-driven industries.

4. **Health Care and Social Assistance:** The health care sector also saw growth, with its GRP rising from $15.38 billion in 2019 to $20.21 billion in 2023. This growth reflects the increasing demand for health services and the sector's resilience.

5. **Finance and Insurance:** The finance and insurance sector showed a steady increase, contributing $14.67 billion in 2019 and $19.50 billion in 2023. This sector's growth indicates a robust financial services industry in the region.

**Per Capita GRP:**
The per capita GRP in San Diego County also increased over the period, from $73,347 in 2019 to $94,916 in 2023. This growth in per capita GRP suggests an improvement in the economic well-being of the residents.

**Conclusion:**
San Diego County's GRP has demonstrated robust growth from 2019 to 2023, driven by key sectors such as government, manufacturing, professional services, health care, and finance. The consistent increase in GRP and per capita GRP indicates a strong and resilient regional economy. The county's focus on innovation, technology, and essential services has positioned it well for continued economic prosperity.
