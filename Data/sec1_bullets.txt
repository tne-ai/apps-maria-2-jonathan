The Gross Regional Product (GRP) of San Diego County from 2019 to 2023 shows a consistent upward trend, indicating economic growth over the period. Here's a detailed analysis:

1. **Overall GRP Growth**:
   - In 2019, the GRP was approximately $244.28 billion.
   - By 2023, the GRP increased to approximately $308.71 billion.
   - This represents a total increase of about $64.43 billion over the five-year period.

2. **Annual GRP Growth**:
   - From 2019 to 2020, the GRP increased slightly by approximately $0.54 billion, reaching $244.82 billion.
   - From 2020 to 2021, there was a more significant increase of approximately $24.05 billion, reaching $268.87 billion.
   - From 2021 to 2022, the GRP grew by approximately $27.81 billion, reaching $296.68 billion.
   - From 2022 to 2023, the GRP increased by approximately $12.03 billion, reaching $308.71 billion.

3. **Per Capita GRP**:
   - In 2019, the per capita GRP was approximately $73,347.
   - By 2023, the per capita GRP increased to approximately $94,916.
   - This indicates an increase of about $21,569 in per capita GRP over the five-year period.

4. **Comparison with State and National Levels**:
   - San Diego County's per capita GRP in 2023 ($94,916) is higher than the national average of $77,366 and slightly higher than the California state average of $93,800.
   - This suggests that San Diego County's economic performance per person is stronger than the national average and competitive with the state average.

5. **Industry Contributions**:
   - Key industries contributing to the GRP include Government, Manufacturing, Professional, Scientific, and Technical Services, and Health Care and Social Assistance.
   - The Government sector consistently contributed the most to the GRP, with a notable increase from $45.19 billion in 2019 to $52.92 billion in 2023.
   - The Manufacturing sector also showed significant growth, from $28.26 billion in 2019 to $31.67 billion in 2023.

Overall, San Diego County's economy has shown resilience and growth over the analyzed period, with significant contributions from key industries and a strong per capita GRP performance.
