The Gross Regional Product (GRP) of San Diego County from 2019 to 2023 shows a consistent upward trend, indicating economic growth over the period. Here's a detailed analysis:

1. **2019**: 
   - The GRP for San Diego County was approximately $244.28 billion.
   - The per capita GRP was $73,346.92, based on a population of 3,330,458.

2. **2020**: 
   - The GRP slightly increased to $244.82 billion, despite the challenges posed by the COVID-19 pandemic.
   - The per capita GRP rose to $74,277.60, with a population decrease to 3,296,045. This indicates that while the overall economic output remained stable, the per capita output increased due to a smaller population.

3. **2021**: 
   - The GRP saw a significant increase to $268.87 billion.
   - The per capita GRP jumped to $82,099.95, with the population slightly decreasing to 3,274,954. This reflects a strong economic recovery and growth post-pandemic.

4. **2022**: 
   - The GRP continued to grow, reaching $296.68 billion.
   - The per capita GRP increased to $90,557.10, with a stable population of 3,276,208. This suggests robust economic performance and increased productivity.

5. **2023**: 
   - The GRP further increased to $308.71 billion.
   - The per capita GRP reached $94,915.87, with a population of 3,252,468. The continued increase in per capita GRP indicates sustained economic growth and efficiency.

**Overall Analysis**:
- San Diego County's economy demonstrated resilience and growth from 2019 to 2023, with the GRP increasing by approximately 26.4% over the five-year period.
- The per capita GRP also showed a significant rise, indicating improved economic productivity and output per person.
- The data suggests that San Diego County has effectively navigated economic challenges, including the pandemic, and has maintained a trajectory of economic expansion.
