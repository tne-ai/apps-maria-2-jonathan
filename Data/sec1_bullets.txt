The Gross Regional Product (GRP) analysis for San Diego County from 2019 to 2023 can be summarized as follows:

1. **Overall GRP Growth**:
   - In 2019, the GRP of San Diego County was approximately $244.28 billion.
   - By 2023, the GRP increased to approximately $308.71 billion.
   - This represents a total growth of approximately $64.43 billion over the five-year period.

2. **Annual GRP Growth**:
   - From 2019 to 2020, the GRP increased slightly from $244.28 billion to $244.82 billion, indicating a modest growth despite the challenges posed by the COVID-19 pandemic.
   - From 2020 to 2021, the GRP saw a significant increase to $268.87 billion, reflecting a recovery and growth phase.
   - The growth continued in 2022, with the GRP reaching $296.68 billion.
   - In 2023, the GRP further increased to $308.71 billion.

3. **Per Capita GRP**:
   - The per capita GRP in 2019 was approximately $73,347.
   - By 2023, the per capita GRP increased to approximately $94,916.
   - This indicates a significant increase in economic productivity per person over the five-year period.

4. **Industry Contributions**:
   - Key industries contributing to the GRP include Government, Manufacturing, Professional, Scientific, and Technical Services, and Health Care and Social Assistance.
   - The Government sector consistently contributed the largest share to the GRP, with its contribution increasing from approximately $45.19 billion in 2019 to $52.92 billion in 2023.
   - The Manufacturing sector also showed strong performance, with its contribution increasing from approximately $28.26 billion in 2019 to $31.67 billion in 2023.
   - The Professional, Scientific, and Technical Services sector saw growth from approximately $28.13 billion in 2019 to $37.04 billion in 2023.

5. **Economic Resilience**:
   - Despite the economic disruptions caused by the COVID-19 pandemic, San Diego County demonstrated resilience with a steady recovery and growth in its GRP from 2020 onwards.

In summary, San Diego County experienced robust economic growth between 2019 and 2023, with significant contributions from key industries and an increase in per capita productivity. The county's economy showed resilience and adaptability in the face of global challenges.
