The Gross Regional Product (GRP) analysis for San Diego County from 2019 to 2023 is as follows:

1. **2019**:
   - Total GRP: $244.28 billion
   - Per Capita GRP: $73,346.92

2. **2020**:
   - Total GRP: $244.82 billion
   - Per Capita GRP: $74,277.60
   - Analysis: Despite the challenges posed by the COVID-19 pandemic, San Diego County's GRP saw a slight increase in 2020. The per capita GRP also increased, indicating a resilience in the region's economic output relative to its population.

3. **2021**:
   - Total GRP: $268.87 billion
   - Per Capita GRP: $82,099.95
   - Analysis: The year 2021 marked a significant recovery and growth in the GRP, with an increase of approximately $24.05 billion from the previous year. The per capita GRP also saw a substantial rise, reflecting strong economic performance and recovery efforts.

4. **2022**:
   - Total GRP: $296.68 billion
   - Per Capita GRP: $90,557.10
   - Analysis: The GRP continued its upward trajectory in 2022, with an increase of about $27.81 billion from 2021. The per capita GRP also increased significantly, indicating robust economic growth and productivity in the region.

5. **2023**:
   - Total GRP: $308.71 billion
   - Per Capita GRP: $94,915.87
   - Analysis: In 2023, San Diego County's GRP reached $308.71 billion, marking a continued growth trend. The per capita GRP also increased, suggesting sustained economic expansion and improved economic conditions for the residents.

**Overall Analysis**:
- Over the five-year period from 2019 to 2023, San Diego County's GRP grew from $244.28 billion to $308.71 billion, representing a total increase of $64.43 billion.
- The per capita GRP also showed a consistent upward trend, increasing from $73,346.92 in 2019 to $94,915.87 in 2023.
- This growth indicates a strong and resilient economy in San Diego County, with significant recovery and expansion following the initial impact of the COVID-19 pandemic. The region's economic performance has been robust, with improvements in productivity and economic output relative to its population.
