The Gross Regional Product (GRP) of San Diego County from 2019 to 2023 shows a consistent upward trend, indicating economic growth over this period. Here's a detailed analysis:

1. **2019**: 
   - The total GRP for San Diego County was approximately $244.28 billion.
   - The per capita GRP was $73,346.92, based on a population of 3,330,458.

2. **2020**: 
   - The GRP slightly increased to approximately $244.82 billion.
   - The per capita GRP rose to $74,277.60, despite a slight decrease in population to 3,296,045. This suggests that the economic output per person increased, even though the overall GRP growth was marginal.

3. **2021**: 
   - The GRP saw a significant increase to approximately $268.87 billion.
   - The per capita GRP jumped to $82,099.95, with the population slightly decreasing to 3,274,954. This indicates a strong economic recovery or growth phase, likely influenced by post-pandemic economic activities.

4. **2022**: 
   - The GRP continued to grow, reaching approximately $296.68 billion.
   - The per capita GRP increased to $90,557.10, with a stable population of 3,276,208. This reflects sustained economic expansion and increased productivity.

5. **2023**: 
   - The GRP further increased to approximately $308.71 billion.
   - The per capita GRP reached $94,915.87, with a population of 3,252,468. This continued growth suggests robust economic health and possibly the impact of strategic economic policies or investments.

**Overall Analysis**:
- Over the five-year period, San Diego County's GRP increased by approximately $64.43 billion, from $244.28 billion in 2019 to $308.71 billion in 2023.
- The per capita GRP increased by $21,568.95, from $73,346.92 in 2019 to $94,915.87 in 2023.
- The consistent increase in both total and per capita GRP indicates a strong and growing economy, with increased productivity and economic output per individual.
- The slight fluctuations in population did not hinder economic growth, suggesting that the region's economic activities were robust enough to sustain and enhance economic output.

This analysis highlights San Diego County's economic resilience and growth trajectory over the past five years.
