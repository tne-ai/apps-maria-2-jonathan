The Gross Regional Product (GRP) of San Diego County from 2019 to 2023 shows a consistent upward trend, indicating economic growth over this period. Here's a detailed analysis:

1. **Overall GRP Growth**:
   - In 2019, the GRP was approximately $244.28 billion.
   - By 2023, the GRP had increased to approximately $308.71 billion.
   - This represents a total increase of about $64.43 billion over the five-year period.

2. **Annual GRP Growth**:
   - From 2019 to 2020, the GRP increased slightly from $244.28 billion to $244.82 billion, showing resilience despite the global economic challenges posed by the COVID-19 pandemic.
   - From 2020 to 2021, the GRP saw a more significant increase to $268.87 billion, indicating a recovery phase.
   - The growth continued in 2022, with the GRP reaching $296.68 billion.
   - By 2023, the GRP further increased to $308.71 billion.

3. **Per Capita GRP**:
   - In 2019, the per capita GRP was approximately $73,347.
   - By 2023, the per capita GRP had risen to approximately $94,916.
   - This increase in per capita GRP suggests that the economic growth outpaced population growth, leading to higher economic output per person.

4. **Industry Contributions**:
   - Key industries contributing to the GRP include Government, Manufacturing, Professional, Scientific, and Technical Services, and Health Care and Social Assistance.
   - The Government sector consistently contributed the largest share, with its contribution increasing from approximately $45.19 billion in 2019 to $52.92 billion in 2023.
   - Manufacturing also showed strong performance, with its contribution rising from approximately $28.26 billion in 2019 to $31.67 billion in 2023.
   - The Professional, Scientific, and Technical Services sector saw significant growth, increasing from approximately $28.13 billion in 2019 to $37.04 billion in 2023.

5. **Economic Resilience and Recovery**:
   - The data indicates that San Diego County's economy demonstrated resilience during the pandemic, with a quick recovery and continued growth in subsequent years.
   - The diversification of the economy, with strong contributions from various sectors, likely played a role in this resilience.

In summary, San Diego County experienced robust economic growth from 2019 to 2023, with significant increases in both total and per capita GRP. The county's diverse economic base, including strong sectors like Government, Manufacturing, and Professional Services, contributed to this positive trend.
