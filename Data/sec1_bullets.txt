The Gross Regional Product (GRP) of San Diego County from 2019 to 2023 shows a consistent upward trend, indicating economic growth over the period. Here's a detailed analysis:

1. **2019**: 
   - The GRP for San Diego County was approximately $244.28 billion.
   - This year serves as the baseline for our analysis.

2. **2020**: 
   - The GRP slightly increased to approximately $244.82 billion.
   - Despite the challenges posed by the COVID-19 pandemic, San Diego County managed to maintain its economic output, showing resilience.

3. **2021**: 
   - The GRP saw a significant increase to approximately $268.87 billion.
   - This growth can be attributed to the recovery from the pandemic, with industries such as Accommodation and Food Services, and Arts, Entertainment, and Recreation beginning to rebound.

4. **2022**: 
   - The GRP continued to grow, reaching approximately $296.68 billion.
   - The increase reflects ongoing economic recovery and expansion, with sectors like Professional, Scientific, and Technical Services, and Manufacturing contributing significantly to the growth.

5. **2023**: 
   - The GRP further increased to approximately $308.71 billion.
   - This growth indicates a robust economic environment, with continued contributions from key sectors such as Government, Health Care and Social Assistance, and Information.

**Per Capita GRP Analysis**:
- The per capita GRP also showed an upward trend, increasing from $73,346.92 in 2019 to $94,915.87 in 2023. This indicates that the economic growth outpaced population growth, leading to higher economic output per person.

**Key Observations**:
- The consistent growth in GRP over the years suggests a strong and resilient economy in San Diego County.
- The recovery post-2020 highlights the adaptability and strength of the local economy, with significant contributions from diverse sectors.
- The increase in per capita GRP suggests improvements in productivity and economic well-being for the residents of San Diego County.

Overall, San Diego County's economy has demonstrated robust growth and resilience from 2019 to 2023, with a diverse range of industries contributing to its economic success.
