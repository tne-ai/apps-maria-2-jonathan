The Gross Regional Product (GRP) of San Diego County from 2019 to 2023 shows a consistent upward trend, indicating economic growth over the period. Here's a detailed analysis:

1. **2019**: 
   - The GRP for San Diego County was approximately $244.28 billion.
   - The per capita GRP was $73,346.92, calculated by dividing the total GRP by the population of 3,330,458.

2. **2020**: 
   - The GRP slightly increased to approximately $244.82 billion.
   - The per capita GRP rose to $74,277.60, despite a decrease in population to 3,296,045. This indicates that the economic output per person increased, even though the overall GRP growth was minimal.

3. **2021**: 
   - The GRP saw a significant increase to approximately $268.87 billion.
   - The per capita GRP jumped to $82,099.95, with the population slightly decreasing to 3,274,954. This reflects a strong economic recovery and growth in productivity per individual.

4. **2022**: 
   - The GRP continued to grow, reaching approximately $296.68 billion.
   - The per capita GRP increased to $90,557.10, with the population at 3,276,208. This indicates sustained economic expansion and increased economic output per person.

5. **2023**: 
   - The GRP further increased to approximately $308.71 billion.
   - The per capita GRP reached $94,915.87, with the population decreasing slightly to 3,252,468. This suggests continued economic growth and a higher economic output per individual.

**Overall Analysis**:
- San Diego County's GRP has shown a consistent increase from 2019 to 2023, with a total growth of approximately $64.43 billion over the five-year period.
- The per capita GRP has also increased significantly, indicating that the economic output per person has improved, reflecting enhanced productivity and economic health.
- The population has shown a slight decline over the years, but this has not hindered the growth in GRP, suggesting that the county's economy has become more efficient and productive.

This analysis highlights the robust economic growth in San Diego County, driven by various sectors contributing to the overall increase in GRP.
