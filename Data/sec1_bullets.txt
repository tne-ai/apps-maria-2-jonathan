The Gross Regional Product (GRP) of San Diego County from 2019 to 2023 shows a consistent upward trend, indicating economic growth over this period. Here's a detailed analysis:

1. **2019**: 
   - The total GRP for San Diego County was approximately $244.28 billion.
   - The per capita GRP was $73,346.92, based on a population of 3,330,458.

2. **2020**: 
   - The GRP slightly increased to approximately $244.82 billion.
   - The per capita GRP rose to $74,277.60, despite a decrease in population to 3,296,045. This suggests that the economic output per person increased, possibly due to productivity gains or shifts in industry contributions.

3. **2021**: 
   - The GRP saw a significant increase to approximately $268.87 billion.
   - The per capita GRP jumped to $82,099.95, with the population slightly decreasing to 3,274,954. This indicates a robust economic recovery or expansion, likely driven by key industries.

4. **2022**: 
   - The GRP continued to grow, reaching approximately $296.68 billion.
   - The per capita GRP increased to $90,557.10, with a stable population of 3,276,208. This growth reflects strong performance across various sectors, contributing to higher economic output.

5. **2023**: 
   - The GRP further increased to approximately $308.71 billion.
   - The per capita GRP reached $94,915.87, with a population of 3,252,468. This continued growth suggests sustained economic development and possibly increased investments or innovations within the region.

**Overall Analysis**:
- Over the five-year period, San Diego County's GRP increased by approximately $64.43 billion, reflecting a strong and resilient economy.
- The per capita GRP increased by $21,568.95, indicating that the economic output per person has grown significantly, which is a positive sign of economic health and productivity.
- The consistent growth in GRP and per capita GRP suggests that San Diego County has been successful in leveraging its economic strengths, possibly in sectors like technology, healthcare, and tourism, to drive economic expansion.
