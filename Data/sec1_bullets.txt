The Gross Regional Product (GRP) analysis for San Diego County from 2019 to 2023 is as follows:

1. **2019**:
   - Total GRP: $244.28 billion
   - Per Capita GRP: $73,346.92
   - Key Industries:
     - Government: $45.19 billion
     - Manufacturing: $28.26 billion
     - Professional, Scientific, and Technical Services: $28.13 billion

2. **2020**:
   - Total GRP: $244.82 billion (a slight increase from 2019)
   - Per Capita GRP: $74,277.60
   - Key Industries:
     - Government: $46.58 billion
     - Manufacturing: $28.83 billion
     - Professional, Scientific, and Technical Services: $29.10 billion
   - Notable Impact: The Accommodation and Food Services industry saw a significant decline from $9.02 billion in 2019 to $6.53 billion in 2020, likely due to the COVID-19 pandemic.

3. **2021**:
   - Total GRP: $268.87 billion (an increase from 2020)
   - Per Capita GRP: $82,099.95
   - Key Industries:
     - Government: $49.23 billion
     - Manufacturing: $31.28 billion
     - Professional, Scientific, and Technical Services: $32.51 billion
   - Recovery: The Accommodation and Food Services industry rebounded to $8.80 billion.

4. **2022**:
   - Total GRP: $296.68 billion (a significant increase from 2021)
   - Per Capita GRP: $90,557.10
   - Key Industries:
     - Government: $50.56 billion
     - Manufacturing: $33.50 billion
     - Professional, Scientific, and Technical Services: $35.74 billion
   - Growth: The Accommodation and Food Services industry continued to grow, reaching $11.77 billion.

5. **2023**:
   - Total GRP: $308.71 billion (continued growth from 2022)
   - Per Capita GRP: $94,915.87
   - Key Industries:
     - Government: $52.92 billion
     - Manufacturing: $31.67 billion
     - Professional, Scientific, and Technical Services: $37.04 billion
   - Stability: The Accommodation and Food Services industry maintained its growth at $11.83 billion.

**Overall Analysis**:
- San Diego County's GRP showed a consistent upward trend from 2019 to 2023, with a notable recovery and growth post-2020.
- The Government sector remained the largest contributor to the GRP throughout the period.
- The Manufacturing and Professional, Scientific, and Technical Services industries also played significant roles in the county's economic performance.
- The Accommodation and Food Services industry experienced a sharp decline in 2020 due to the pandemic but showed resilience and recovery in the subsequent years.
