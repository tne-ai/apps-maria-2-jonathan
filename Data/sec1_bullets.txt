The Gross Regional Product (GRP) analysis for San Diego County from 2019 to 2023 is as follows:

1. **Overall GRP Growth**:
   - In 2019, the GRP of San Diego County was approximately $244.28 billion.
   - By 2023, the GRP increased to approximately $308.71 billion.
   - This represents a total increase of approximately $64.43 billion over the five-year period.

2. **Annual GRP Growth**:
   - From 2019 to 2020, the GRP increased slightly from $244.28 billion to $244.82 billion, showing resilience despite the global economic challenges posed by the COVID-19 pandemic.
   - From 2020 to 2021, the GRP saw a significant increase to $268.87 billion, indicating a strong economic recovery.
   - The growth continued in 2022, with the GRP reaching $296.68 billion.
   - In 2023, the GRP further increased to $308.71 billion.

3. **Per Capita GRP**:
   - In 2019, the GRP per capita was approximately $73,347.
   - By 2023, the GRP per capita increased to approximately $94,916.
   - This indicates a substantial increase in economic productivity per person over the five-year period.

4. **Comparison with State and National Levels**:
   - San Diego County's GRP per capita in 2023 ($94,916) was higher than the state average of California, which was approximately $93,800.
   - It was also significantly higher than the national average, which was approximately $77,366 in 2023.

5. **Sectoral Contributions**:
   - Key sectors contributing to the GRP included Government, Manufacturing, and Professional, Scientific, and Technical Services.
   - The Government sector consistently contributed the highest to the GRP, with $52.92 billion in 2023.
   - Manufacturing and Professional, Scientific, and Technical Services also showed strong contributions, with $31.67 billion and $37.04 billion respectively in 2023.

Overall, San Diego County demonstrated robust economic growth from 2019 to 2023, with significant increases in both total GRP and GRP per capita, outperforming both state and national averages. The county's economy showed resilience and adaptability, particularly in the face of global economic challenges.
