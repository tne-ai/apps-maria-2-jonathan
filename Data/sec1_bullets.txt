### Slide 3: GRP Overview
- **Definition:** Gross Regional Product (GRP) is the total economic output of a region.
- **Importance:** It measures the economic performance and health of a region.

### Slide 4: San Diego County GRP (2019-2023)
- **2019 GRP:** $244.28 billion
- **2020 GRP:** $244.82 billion
- **2021 GRP:** $268.87 billion
- **2022 GRP:** $296.68 billion
- **2023 GRP:** $308.71 billion

### Slide 5: GRP Growth Analysis
- **2019-2020:** Minimal growth due to economic challenges.
- **2020-2021:** Significant increase of 9.8% as the economy began to recover.
- **2021-2022:** Continued growth of 10.3% driven by key industries.
- **2022-2023:** Growth slowed to 4.1%, indicating stabilization.

### Slide 6: Key Industries Contributing to GRP
- **Government:** Largest contributor with consistent growth.
- **Manufacturing:** Significant growth, especially in 2021 and 2022.
- **Professional, Scientific, and Technical Services:** Steady growth across all years.
- **Health Care and Social Assistance:** Consistent contributor to GRP.

### Slide 7: Per Capita GRP Analysis
- **2019 Per Capita GRP:** $73,347
- **2020 Per Capita GRP:** $74,278
- **2021 Per Capita GRP:** $82,100
- **2022 Per Capita GRP:** $90,557
- **2023 Per Capita GRP:** $94,916

### Slide 8: Comparison with State and National Averages
- **California Per Capita GRP (2023):** $93,800
- **National Per Capita GRP (2023):** $77,366
- **Insight:** San Diego County's per capita GRP is higher than both state and national averages.

### Slide 9: Conclusion
- **Summary:** San Diego County's GRP has shown robust growth from 2019 to 2023, with significant contributions from government, manufacturing, and professional services.
- **Future Outlook:** Continued focus on key industries can sustain economic growth.
