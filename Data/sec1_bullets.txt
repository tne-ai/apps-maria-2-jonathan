The Gross Regional Product (GRP) of San Diego County from 2019 to 2023 shows a consistent upward trend, indicating economic growth over the period. Here's a detailed analysis:

1. **2019**: 
   - The GRP for San Diego County was approximately $244.28 billion.
   - The per capita GRP was $73,346.92, based on a population of 3,330,458.

2. **2020**: 
   - The GRP slightly increased to approximately $244.82 billion.
   - The per capita GRP rose to $74,277.60, despite a slight decrease in population to 3,296,045. This suggests that the economic output per person increased, even though the overall GRP growth was minimal.

3. **2021**: 
   - The GRP saw a more significant increase to approximately $268.87 billion.
   - The per capita GRP jumped to $82,099.95, with the population slightly decreasing to 3,274,954. This indicates a strong recovery and growth in economic output per person.

4. **2022**: 
   - The GRP continued to grow, reaching approximately $296.68 billion.
   - The per capita GRP increased to $90,557.10, with a population of 3,276,208. This reflects continued economic expansion and increased productivity.

5. **2023**: 
   - The GRP further increased to approximately $308.71 billion.
   - The per capita GRP reached $94,915.87, with the population slightly decreasing to 3,252,468. This suggests that the economic output per person continued to rise, indicating robust economic health.

**Overall Analysis**:
- Over the five-year period, San Diego County's GRP increased by approximately $64.43 billion, from $244.28 billion in 2019 to $308.71 billion in 2023.
- The per capita GRP increased by $21,568.95, from $73,346.92 in 2019 to $94,915.87 in 2023.
- The consistent increase in both total GRP and per capita GRP suggests strong economic growth and increased productivity in San Diego County over the period.
- The slight decrease in population from 2019 to 2023 did not hinder economic growth, as evidenced by the rising per capita GRP, indicating that the region's economic output per individual improved significantly.
