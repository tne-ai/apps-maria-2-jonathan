The Gross Regional Product (GRP) of San Diego County from 2019 to 2023 shows a consistent upward trend, indicating economic growth over the period. Here's a detailed analysis:

1. **Overall GRP Growth**:
   - In 2019, the GRP was approximately $244.28 billion.
   - By 2023, the GRP increased to approximately $308.71 billion.
   - This represents an increase of about $64.43 billion over the five-year period.

2. **Annual GRP Figures**:
   - 2019: $244.28 billion
   - 2020: $244.82 billion
   - 2021: $268.87 billion
   - 2022: $296.68 billion
   - 2023: $308.71 billion

3. **Yearly Growth Rates**:
   - From 2019 to 2020, the GRP increased by approximately 0.22%.
   - From 2020 to 2021, the GRP saw a significant increase of approximately 9.82%.
   - From 2021 to 2022, the GRP grew by approximately 10.34%.
   - From 2022 to 2023, the GRP increased by approximately 4.05%.

4. **Per Capita GRP**:
   - The per capita GRP also showed growth, indicating that the economic growth was not solely due to population increase.
   - 2019: $73,346.92
   - 2020: $74,277.60
   - 2021: $82,099.95
   - 2022: $90,557.10
   - 2023: $94,915.87

5. **Key Observations**:
   - The GRP growth was relatively modest from 2019 to 2020, likely impacted by the global economic slowdown due to the COVID-19 pandemic.
   - The significant growth from 2020 to 2022 suggests a strong economic recovery and expansion.
   - The per capita GRP growth indicates that the economic gains were not just due to population growth but also due to increased productivity and economic activity.

Overall, San Diego County's economy demonstrated resilience and growth over the five-year period, with notable recovery and expansion post-2020.
