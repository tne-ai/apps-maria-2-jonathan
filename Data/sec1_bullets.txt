The Gross Regional Product (GRP) analysis for San Diego County from 2019 to 2023 is as follows:

1. **2019**:
   - Total GRP: $244.28 billion
   - Per Capita GRP: $73,346.92

2. **2020**:
   - Total GRP: $244.82 billion
   - Per Capita GRP: $74,277.60
   - Despite the challenges posed by the COVID-19 pandemic, San Diego County's GRP saw a slight increase in 2020. This resilience can be attributed to the diverse economic base of the region, which includes strong sectors such as healthcare, government, and finance.

3. **2021**:
   - Total GRP: $268.87 billion
   - Per Capita GRP: $82,099.95
   - The year 2021 marked a significant recovery and growth in the GRP, with an increase of approximately 9.8% from the previous year. This growth reflects the economic rebound as restrictions eased and businesses adapted to the new normal.

4. **2022**:
   - Total GRP: $296.68 billion
   - Per Capita GRP: $90,557.10
   - The GRP continued its upward trajectory in 2022, with a growth rate of about 10.3% from 2021. This growth can be attributed to the continued expansion in key industries such as professional, scientific, and technical services, as well as manufacturing.

5. **2023**:
   - Total GRP: $308.71 billion
   - Per Capita GRP: $94,915.87
   - In 2023, the GRP growth rate slowed to approximately 4.1%, indicating a stabilization of the economic recovery. The per capita GRP also increased, reflecting the overall economic prosperity of the region.

**Overall Analysis**:
- Over the five-year period, San Diego County's GRP grew from $244.28 billion in 2019 to $308.71 billion in 2023, representing a total growth of approximately 26.4%.
- The per capita GRP increased from $73,346.92 in 2019 to $94,915.87 in 2023, indicating a significant improvement in economic output per resident.
- The data suggests that San Diego County has a robust and resilient economy, capable of recovering from economic disruptions and continuing to grow. The key sectors driving this growth include government, healthcare, professional services, and manufacturing.
