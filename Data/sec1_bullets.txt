The Gross Regional Product (GRP) analysis for San Diego County between 2019 and 2023 is as follows:

1. **Overall GRP Growth**:
   - In 2019, the GRP of San Diego County was approximately $244.28 billion.
   - By 2023, the GRP increased to approximately $308.71 billion.
   - This represents a total increase of approximately $64.43 billion over the five-year period.

2. **Annual GRP Growth**:
   - From 2019 to 2020, the GRP increased slightly from $244.28 billion to $244.82 billion, showing resilience despite the global economic challenges posed by the COVID-19 pandemic.
   - From 2020 to 2021, the GRP saw a significant increase to $268.87 billion, indicating a strong recovery.
   - The growth continued in 2022, with the GRP reaching $296.68 billion.
   - In 2023, the GRP further increased to $308.71 billion.

3. **Per Capita GRP**:
   - In 2019, the per capita GRP was approximately $73,347.
   - By 2023, the per capita GRP increased to approximately $94,916.
   - This indicates a significant improvement in economic productivity per person over the period.

4. **Industry Contributions**:
   - The Government sector consistently contributed the most to the GRP, with its contribution increasing from approximately $45.19 billion in 2019 to $52.92 billion in 2023.
   - The Manufacturing sector also played a significant role, with its contribution rising from approximately $28.26 billion in 2019 to $31.67 billion in 2023.
   - The Professional, Scientific, and Technical Services sector showed notable growth, increasing from approximately $28.13 billion in 2019 to $37.04 billion in 2023.
   - The Health Care and Social Assistance sector also saw growth, with its contribution rising from approximately $15.38 billion in 2019 to $20.21 billion in 2023.

5. **Economic Resilience and Recovery**:
   - The data indicates that San Diego County's economy demonstrated resilience during the pandemic, with a quick recovery and continued growth in subsequent years.
   - The increase in GRP and per capita GRP suggests improvements in both overall economic output and individual economic productivity.

In summary, San Diego County experienced robust economic growth between 2019 and 2023, with significant contributions from key sectors such as Government, Manufacturing, and Professional Services. The increase in per capita GRP also highlights improvements in economic productivity.
