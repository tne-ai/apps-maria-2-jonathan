The Gross Regional Product (GRP) of San Diego County from 2019 to 2023 shows a consistent upward trend, indicating economic growth over the period. Here's a detailed analysis:

1. **2019**: 
   - The GRP for San Diego County was approximately $244.28 billion.
   - The per capita GRP was $73,346.92, calculated by dividing the total GRP by the population of 3,330,458.

2. **2020**: 
   - The GRP slightly increased to approximately $244.82 billion.
   - The per capita GRP rose to $74,277.60, despite a decrease in population to 3,296,045. This suggests that the economic output per person increased, possibly due to productivity gains or shifts in industry contributions.

3. **2021**: 
   - The GRP saw a significant increase to approximately $268.87 billion.
   - The per capita GRP jumped to $82,099.95, with the population slightly decreasing to 3,274,954. This indicates a robust economic recovery or expansion, likely driven by key industries.

4. **2022**: 
   - The GRP continued to grow, reaching approximately $296.68 billion.
   - The per capita GRP increased to $90,557.10, with a stable population of 3,276,208. This reflects strong economic performance and possibly increased investment or innovation in the region.

5. **2023**: 
   - The GRP further increased to approximately $308.71 billion.
   - The per capita GRP reached $94,915.87, with a population of 3,252,468. This continued growth suggests sustained economic development and resilience in the face of potential challenges.

**Overall Analysis**:
- San Diego County's GRP has shown consistent growth from 2019 to 2023, with a notable increase in per capita GRP each year. 
- The economic expansion is likely supported by key industries such as Professional, Scientific, and Technical Services, Manufacturing, and Government, which have shown strong performance in the region.
- The increase in per capita GRP, despite fluctuations in population, indicates improved economic productivity and possibly higher value-added activities within the county. 

This analysis highlights San Diego County's economic resilience and growth potential over the analyzed period.
