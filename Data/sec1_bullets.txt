The Gross Regional Product (GRP) analysis for San Diego County from 2019 to 2023 is as follows:

1. **2019**:
   - Total GRP: $244.28 billion
   - Population: 3,330,458
   - GRP per Capita: $73,346.92

2. **2020**:
   - Total GRP: $244.82 billion
   - Population: 3,296,045
   - GRP per Capita: $74,277.60
   - Analysis: Despite the challenges posed by the COVID-19 pandemic, San Diego County's GRP saw a slight increase from 2019. The GRP per capita also increased, indicating a relatively stable economic performance during a challenging year.

3. **2021**:
   - Total GRP: $268.87 billion
   - Population: 3,274,954
   - GRP per Capita: $82,099.95
   - Analysis: The year 2021 marked a significant recovery and growth in the GRP, with an increase of approximately $24.05 billion from 2020. The GRP per capita also saw a substantial rise, reflecting economic recovery and growth post-pandemic.

4. **2022**:
   - Total GRP: $296.68 billion
   - Population: 3,276,208
   - GRP per Capita: $90,557.10
   - Analysis: The GRP continued to grow in 2022, with an increase of about $27.81 billion from 2021. The GRP per capita also increased significantly, indicating strong economic performance and growth in the region.

5. **2023**:
   - Total GRP: $308.71 billion
   - Population: 3,252,468
   - GRP per Capita: $94,915.87
   - Analysis: In 2023, San Diego County's GRP reached $308.71 billion, marking a continued upward trend. The GRP per capita also increased, suggesting sustained economic growth and prosperity in the region.

**Overall Analysis**:
- Over the five-year period from 2019 to 2023, San Diego County's GRP increased by approximately $64.43 billion, reflecting a strong and resilient economy.
- The GRP per capita also showed a consistent upward trend, increasing by $21,568.95 over the same period, indicating improved economic conditions and productivity per individual in the county.
- The data suggests that San Diego County has experienced robust economic growth, particularly in the post-pandemic years, with significant increases in both total GRP and GRP per capita.
