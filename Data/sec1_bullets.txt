To analyze the Gross Regional Product (GRP) of San Diego County between 2019 and 2023, we will examine the data provided in the CSV files. The analysis will focus on the total GRP, per capita GRP, and the performance of various industries over the years.

### Total GRP Analysis

1. **2019**: The total GRP for San Diego County was approximately $244.28 billion.
2. **2020**: The GRP slightly increased to approximately $244.82 billion, despite the economic challenges posed by the COVID-19 pandemic.
3. **2021**: The GRP saw a significant increase to approximately $268.87 billion, indicating a recovery and growth phase.
4. **2022**: The GRP continued to grow, reaching approximately $296.68 billion.
5. **2023**: The GRP further increased to approximately $308.71 billion.

### Per Capita GRP Analysis

1. **2019**: The per capita GRP was approximately $73,347.
2. **2020**: The per capita GRP increased slightly to approximately $74,278.
3. **2021**: There was a notable increase in per capita GRP to approximately $82,100.
4. **2022**: The per capita GRP rose to approximately $90,557.
5. **2023**: The per capita GRP reached approximately $94,916.

### Industry Performance

- **Accommodation and Food Services**: This industry experienced fluctuations, with a decline in 2020 due to the pandemic but showed recovery in subsequent years.
- **Administrative and Support and Waste Management and Remediation Services**: This sector showed consistent growth over the years.
- **Agriculture, Forestry, Fishing and Hunting**: This industry had a steady increase in GRP contribution.
- **Arts, Entertainment, and Recreation**: This sector was heavily impacted in 2020 but showed recovery in the following years.
- **Construction**: The construction industry showed consistent growth throughout the period.
- **Finance and Insurance**: This sector experienced significant growth, especially in 2021 and 2022.
- **Government**: The government sector remained the largest contributor to the GRP, with consistent growth each year.
- **Health Care and Social Assistance**: This sector showed steady growth, reflecting the increasing demand for healthcare services.
- **Information**: The information sector saw growth, particularly in 2022 and 2023.
- **Manufacturing**: Manufacturing remained a strong contributor, with growth observed in 2021 and 2022.
- **Professional, Scientific, and Technical Services**: This sector showed significant growth, particularly in 2022 and 2023.
- **Real Estate and Rental and Leasing**: This industry experienced growth, especially in 2022 and 2023.
- **Retail Trade**: Retail trade showed growth, with a notable increase in 2023.
- **Transportation and Warehousing**: This sector experienced growth, particularly in 2022 and 2023.

### Conclusion

San Diego County's GRP showed resilience and growth from 2019 to 2023, with a notable recovery from the pandemic's impact in 2020. The per capita GRP also increased significantly, indicating economic growth and improved productivity. Key industries such as government, professional services, and manufacturing played a significant role in driving this growth.
