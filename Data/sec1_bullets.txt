The Gross Regional Product (GRP) of San Diego County from 2019 to 2023 shows a consistent upward trend, indicating economic growth over this period. Here's a detailed analysis:

1. **Overall GRP Growth**:
   - In 2019, the GRP was approximately $244.28 billion.
   - By 2023, the GRP had increased to approximately $308.71 billion.
   - This represents a total increase of approximately $64.43 billion over the five-year period.

2. **Annual GRP Growth**:
   - From 2019 to 2020, the GRP increased slightly by approximately $0.54 billion, reaching $244.82 billion.
   - From 2020 to 2021, the GRP saw a more significant increase of approximately $24.05 billion, reaching $268.87 billion.
   - From 2021 to 2022, the GRP increased by approximately $27.81 billion, reaching $296.68 billion.
   - From 2022 to 2023, the GRP increased by approximately $12.03 billion, reaching $308.71 billion.

3. **Per Capita GRP**:
   - In 2019, the per capita GRP was approximately $73,347.
   - By 2023, the per capita GRP had increased to approximately $94,916.
   - This indicates an increase in economic productivity per person over the period.

4. **Comparison with State and National Levels**:
   - San Diego County's per capita GRP in 2023 ($94,916) was higher than the state average of California ($93,800) and significantly higher than the national average ($77,366).
   - This suggests that San Diego County's economy is performing well relative to both the state and national levels.

5. **Sectoral Contributions**:
   - The largest contributors to the GRP in 2023 were the Government sector ($52.92 billion), Professional, Scientific, and Technical Services ($37.04 billion), and Manufacturing ($31.67 billion).
   - These sectors have consistently been major contributors to the county's economy over the years.

In summary, San Diego County's economy has shown robust growth from 2019 to 2023, with significant increases in both total and per capita GRP. The county's economic performance has outpaced both state and national averages, driven by strong contributions from key sectors such as government, professional services, and manufacturing.
