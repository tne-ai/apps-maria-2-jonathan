The Gross Regional Product (GRP) analysis for San Diego County from 2019 to 2023 is as follows:

1. **Overall GRP Growth**:
   - In 2019, the GRP of San Diego County was approximately $244.28 billion.
   - By 2023, the GRP increased to approximately $308.71 billion.
   - This represents a total growth of approximately $64.43 billion over the five-year period.

2. **Annual GRP Growth**:
   - From 2019 to 2020, the GRP increased slightly from $244.28 billion to $244.82 billion, indicating a modest growth despite the challenges posed by the COVID-19 pandemic.
   - From 2020 to 2021, the GRP saw a more significant increase to $268.87 billion, reflecting a recovery phase.
   - The growth continued in 2022, with the GRP reaching $296.68 billion.
   - By 2023, the GRP further increased to $308.71 billion.

3. **Per Capita GRP**:
   - In 2019, the per capita GRP was approximately $73,347.
   - By 2023, the per capita GRP rose to approximately $94,916.
   - This indicates an increase in economic productivity per person over the period.

4. **Comparison with State and National Levels**:
   - San Diego County's per capita GRP in 2023 ($94,916) was higher than the national average of $77,366 and slightly higher than the California state average of $93,800.
   - This suggests that San Diego County's economic performance per person is relatively strong compared to both state and national levels.

5. **Sectoral Contributions**:
   - Key sectors contributing to the GRP include Government, Manufacturing, and Professional, Scientific, and Technical Services.
   - The Government sector consistently contributed the highest dollar value to the GRP each year, with $52.92 billion in 2023.
   - Manufacturing and Professional, Scientific, and Technical Services also showed significant contributions, with $31.67 billion and $37.04 billion respectively in 2023.

Overall, San Diego County experienced steady economic growth from 2019 to 2023, with significant contributions from key sectors and a strong per capita GRP performance compared to state and national averages.
