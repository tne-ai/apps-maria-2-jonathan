The Gross Regional Product (GRP) of San Diego County from 2019 to 2023 shows a consistent upward trend, indicating economic growth over the period. Here's a detailed analysis:

1. **2019**: 
   - The total GRP for San Diego County was approximately $244.28 billion.
   - The per capita GRP was $73,346.92, calculated by dividing the total GRP by the population of 3,330,458.

2. **2020**: 
   - The GRP slightly increased to approximately $244.82 billion.
   - The per capita GRP rose to $74,277.60, despite a decrease in population to 3,296,045. This suggests that the economic output per person increased, even though the overall economic growth was modest.

3. **2021**: 
   - There was a significant increase in GRP to approximately $268.87 billion.
   - The per capita GRP jumped to $82,099.95, with the population slightly decreasing to 3,274,954. This indicates a robust economic recovery and growth during this year.

4. **2022**: 
   - The GRP continued to grow, reaching approximately $296.68 billion.
   - The per capita GRP increased to $90,557.10, with a population of 3,276,208. This reflects continued economic expansion and increased productivity per individual.

5. **2023**: 
   - The GRP further increased to approximately $308.71 billion.
   - The per capita GRP reached $94,915.87, with the population slightly decreasing to 3,252,468. This suggests sustained economic growth and a high level of economic output per person.

**Overall Analysis**:
- Over the five-year period, San Diego County's GRP increased by approximately $64.43 billion, from $244.28 billion in 2019 to $308.71 billion in 2023.
- The per capita GRP saw a substantial increase of $21,568.95, from $73,346.92 in 2019 to $94,915.87 in 2023.
- The consistent growth in both total and per capita GRP indicates a strong and resilient economy in San Diego County, with increasing economic productivity and output per individual over the years.
