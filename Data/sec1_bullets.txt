The Gross Regional Product (GRP) of San Diego County from 2019 to 2023 shows a consistent upward trend, indicating economic growth over the period. Here's a detailed analysis:

1. **2019**: 
   - The GRP for San Diego County was approximately $244.28 billion.
   - The per capita GRP was $73,346.92, based on a population of 3,330,458.

2. **2020**: 
   - The GRP slightly increased to approximately $244.82 billion.
   - The per capita GRP rose to $74,277.60, despite a decrease in population to 3,296,045. This suggests that the economic output per person increased, even though the overall population decreased.

3. **2021**: 
   - The GRP saw a significant increase to approximately $268.87 billion.
   - The per capita GRP jumped to $82,099.95, with the population slightly decreasing to 3,274,954. This indicates a strong economic recovery and growth post-2020.

4. **2022**: 
   - The GRP continued to grow, reaching approximately $296.68 billion.
   - The per capita GRP increased to $90,557.10, with a population of 3,276,208. This reflects continued economic expansion and increased productivity.

5. **2023**: 
   - The GRP further increased to approximately $308.71 billion.
   - The per capita GRP reached $94,915.87, with the population slightly decreasing to 3,252,468. This suggests that the economic output per person continued to rise, indicating improved efficiency and productivity in the region.

**Overall Analysis**:
- San Diego County's GRP has shown consistent growth from 2019 to 2023, with a notable increase in per capita GRP each year.
- The economic growth is evident despite fluctuations in population, indicating increased productivity and economic resilience.
- The data suggests that San Diego County has been able to enhance its economic output and efficiency over the years, contributing to a higher standard of living and economic prosperity for its residents.
