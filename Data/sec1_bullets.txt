The Gross Regional Product (GRP) of San Diego County from 2019 to 2023 shows a consistent upward trend, indicating economic growth over the period. Here's a detailed analysis:

1. **Overall GRP Growth**:
   - In 2019, the GRP was approximately $244.28 billion.
   - By 2023, the GRP increased to approximately $308.71 billion.
   - This represents an increase of about $64.43 billion over the five-year period.

2. **Annual GRP Figures**:
   - 2019: $244.28 billion
   - 2020: $244.82 billion
   - 2021: $268.87 billion
   - 2022: $296.68 billion
   - 2023: $308.71 billion

3. **Year-over-Year Growth**:
   - From 2019 to 2020, the GRP saw a slight increase of approximately $0.54 billion, which is a modest growth likely impacted by the global economic slowdown due to the COVID-19 pandemic.
   - From 2020 to 2021, the GRP increased by approximately $24.05 billion, indicating a strong recovery.
   - From 2021 to 2022, the GRP grew by approximately $27.81 billion, continuing the recovery trend.
   - From 2022 to 2023, the GRP increased by approximately $12.03 billion, showing sustained growth.

4. **Per Capita GRP**:
   - The per capita GRP also increased over the period, reflecting not only overall economic growth but also an increase in economic output per resident.
   - 2019: $73,346.92
   - 2020: $74,277.60
   - 2021: $82,099.95
   - 2022: $90,557.10
   - 2023: $94,915.87

5. **Comparison with State and National Levels**:
   - San Diego County's per capita GRP in 2023 ($94,915.87) is higher than the state average of California ($93,799.67) and significantly higher than the national average ($77,366.43).
   - This indicates that San Diego County is performing well economically compared to both the state and national levels.

In summary, San Diego County's GRP has shown robust growth from 2019 to 2023, with significant increases in both total GRP and per capita GRP. The county's economic performance is strong relative to state and national averages, highlighting its economic resilience and growth potential.
