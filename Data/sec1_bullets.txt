To analyze the Gross Regional Product (GRP) of San Diego County between 2019 and 2023, we will examine the data provided in the CSV files. The analysis will focus on the total GRP, per capita GRP, and the contributions of various industries to the GRP over the specified period.

### Total GRP Analysis

1. **2019**: The total GRP for San Diego County was approximately $244.28 billion.
2. **2020**: The GRP slightly increased to approximately $244.82 billion, despite the economic challenges posed by the COVID-19 pandemic.
3. **2021**: The GRP saw a significant increase to approximately $268.87 billion, indicating a recovery and growth phase.
4. **2022**: The GRP continued to grow, reaching approximately $296.68 billion.
5. **2023**: The GRP further increased to approximately $308.71 billion.

### Per Capita GRP Analysis

1. **2019**: The per capita GRP was approximately $73,347.
2. **2020**: The per capita GRP increased slightly to approximately $74,278.
3. **2021**: There was a notable increase in per capita GRP to approximately $82,100.
4. **2022**: The per capita GRP rose to approximately $90,557.
5. **2023**: The per capita GRP reached approximately $94,916.

### Industry Contributions

- **Government**: Consistently the largest contributor to the GRP, with values increasing from approximately $45.19 billion in 2019 to $52.92 billion in 2023.
- **Manufacturing**: Another significant contributor, with values ranging from approximately $28.26 billion in 2019 to $31.67 billion in 2023.
- **Professional, Scientific, and Technical Services**: This sector showed growth, contributing approximately $28.13 billion in 2019 and increasing to $37.04 billion in 2023.
- **Health Care and Social Assistance**: This sector also grew, with contributions increasing from approximately $15.38 billion in 2019 to $20.21 billion in 2023.
- **Finance and Insurance**: This sector saw fluctuations but generally increased from approximately $14.67 billion in 2019 to $19.50 billion in 2023.

### Summary

Overall, San Diego County's GRP showed a positive growth trend from 2019 to 2023, with both total and per capita GRP increasing each year. The growth was driven by significant contributions from the government, manufacturing, professional services, health care, and finance sectors. The data indicates a robust economic recovery and expansion following the initial impact of the COVID-19 pandemic in 2020.
