The Gross Regional Product (GRP) analysis for San Diego County between 2019 and 2023 is as follows:

1. **Overall GRP Growth**:
   - In 2019, the GRP for San Diego County was approximately $244.28 billion.
   - By 2023, the GRP had increased to approximately $308.71 billion.
   - This represents a total increase of approximately $64.43 billion over the five-year period.

2. **Annual GRP Growth**:
   - From 2019 to 2020, the GRP increased slightly from $244.28 billion to $244.82 billion, showing resilience despite the global economic challenges posed by the COVID-19 pandemic.
   - From 2020 to 2021, the GRP saw a significant increase to $268.87 billion, indicating a strong economic recovery.
   - The growth continued in 2022, with the GRP reaching $296.68 billion.
   - By 2023, the GRP further increased to $308.71 billion.

3. **Per Capita GRP**:
   - In 2019, the per capita GRP was approximately $73,347.
   - By 2023, the per capita GRP had risen to approximately $94,916.
   - This indicates an increase in economic productivity per person in the region.

4. **Comparison with State and National Levels**:
   - San Diego County's per capita GRP in 2023 ($94,916) was higher than the state average for California ($93,800) and significantly higher than the national average ($77,366).
   - This suggests that San Diego County's economy is performing well relative to both the state and national levels.

5. **Sector Contributions**:
   - Key sectors contributing to the GRP growth include Government, Professional, Scientific, and Technical Services, and Manufacturing.
   - The Government sector consistently contributed the highest dollar value to the GRP each year, with $52.92 billion in 2023.
   - The Professional, Scientific, and Technical Services sector also showed strong growth, reaching $37.04 billion in 2023.
   - Manufacturing remained a significant contributor, with $31.67 billion in 2023.

Overall, San Diego County demonstrated robust economic growth from 2019 to 2023, with significant increases in both total and per capita GRP, outperforming state and national averages. The region's diverse economic base, including strong contributions from government, professional services, and manufacturing sectors, has supported this growth trajectory.
