To analyze the Gross Regional Product (GRP) of San Diego County between 2019 and 2023, we will examine the data provided in the CSV files. The analysis will focus on the total GRP, per capita GRP, and the contributions of various industries to the GRP over the specified period.

### Total GRP Analysis

1. **2019**: The total GRP for San Diego County was approximately $244.28 billion.
2. **2020**: The GRP slightly increased to approximately $244.82 billion, despite the economic challenges posed by the COVID-19 pandemic.
3. **2021**: The GRP saw a significant increase to approximately $268.87 billion, indicating a recovery and growth phase.
4. **2022**: The GRP continued to grow, reaching approximately $296.68 billion.
5. **2023**: The GRP further increased to approximately $308.71 billion.

### Per Capita GRP Analysis

1. **2019**: The per capita GRP was approximately $73,347.
2. **2020**: The per capita GRP increased to approximately $74,278.
3. **2021**: There was a notable rise in per capita GRP to approximately $82,100.
4. **2022**: The per capita GRP further increased to approximately $90,557.
5. **2023**: The per capita GRP reached approximately $94,916.

### Industry Contributions

- **Government**: Consistently the largest contributor, with GRP values increasing from approximately $45.19 billion in 2019 to $52.92 billion in 2023.
- **Manufacturing**: Another significant contributor, with values rising from approximately $28.26 billion in 2019 to $31.67 billion in 2023.
- **Professional, Scientific, and Technical Services**: This sector showed growth from approximately $28.13 billion in 2019 to $37.04 billion in 2023.
- **Health Care and Social Assistance**: Increased from approximately $15.38 billion in 2019 to $20.21 billion in 2023.
- **Finance and Insurance**: Grew from approximately $14.67 billion in 2019 to $19.50 billion in 2023.

### Summary

San Diego County's GRP has shown a consistent upward trend from 2019 to 2023, with both total and per capita GRP increasing each year. The growth reflects economic recovery post-pandemic and expansion in key sectors such as government, manufacturing, professional services, and health care. The per capita GRP growth indicates that the economic benefits are being distributed across the population, contributing to an overall increase in economic well-being in the region.
